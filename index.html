Engineering Design Portfolio

Purpose and Motivation

The purpose of this portfolio is to showcase my knowledge, experiences, and achievements in engineering and engineering design in the last 5 years. I will demonstrate how I learned and applied engineering design through coursework projects as well as extracurricular engagements. Finally, I will go over how the skills I have learned and engineering design projects I have worked on, helped in addressing eBay Canada’s active buyer problem during my fourth-year multi-disciplinary capstone project.

Coursework

Engineering Strategies and Practices I

APS111

My first experience with engineering design was in the first-year engineering strategies and practices (ESP) course where we had to design an engineering solution to transport vaccines in an environment that was not hospitable to it. In a group of 5 other engineers, we identified the primary functions, objectives, and constraints of the design problem. Using what we had learned in class, we attempted to generate innovative ideas through the help of structured brainstorming. After coming up with 5 alternative designs, we selected the most optimal design for the problem by utilizing a weighted decision matrix. From the results we gained, we found that cryo-pack design was the best design due to its ability to meet the functions and constraints, while performing the best on our objectives. This design that I helped create using the principles of engineering design.

Engineering Strategies and Practices II

APS112

In ESPII, I got a chance to apply my learning in solving a problem for a client. Our design was tasked to design a way to teach high school students about engineering design through a fun activity. Again, we leveraged our learnings from class and identified the problem functions, objectives, and constraints to scope the problem. Additionally, we used a pairwise comparison chart to determine the best design out of the dozens of designs we brainstormed as shown in Figure 3. The design we came up with was called the “Angry Birds™ Catapult Design” (ABCD). ABCD was a fun design where participants were challenged to create catapults (Angry Bird™ stations) and use measurements/calculations to aim and fire a projectile at a designated target. It incorporated topics from five courses: Dynamics, Mechanics, Applied Science and Materials; and Engineering Strategies and Practice I & II.

Object-Oriented Programming

MIE250

In second year, the object-oriented programming course helped me develop by planning and programming skills. During the course we were tasked with developing a program that would match students and schools together using the Gale Shapley algorithm. The process of building this java program from scratch, taught me the importance of planning and documenting my design process. Moreover, the project also taught me that in order to build a functional and scalable product, it must be built in parts and modularly.

Human-Centered Design

MIE240

Another course in my second year that affected my view on engineering design was the human centered design course. This course made me realize the importance of designing for people. I also learned that designing for people required a lot of usability testing and design with a purpose driven by principles that had been researched and tested by researchers in the Human Factors field. I worked on a project to improve the user-interface of the Spotify desktop application. In a tea of 3, we leveraged our course knowledge to reduce the number errors in the task of adding a song into your playlist, while also increasing the learnability of the application by changing button location and feedback.

Analytics in Action

MIE465

The course that helped me develop the necessary technical skills to solve the problem provided to us by eBay Canada was Analytics in Action during the second semester of my third year. This course focused on the theory and application of machine learning and data science. A team of 4 industrial engineers, used our learnings to develop a machine learning binary classification model that would predict whether a song would enter the billboard 100 based on its musical characteristics only. The experience working with class imbalance and high-dimensional feature space in this project was essential to our capstone design as they shared many similarities.

eBay Canada Capstone Project

APS490 Multi-Disciplinary Capstone

Sept 2018 - Apr 2019

Improving Buyer Reactivation with Predictive Coupon Targeting

For my graduate capstone, I had the privilege of working with eBay Canada looking for way to growth their active buyer base. This project was the culmination my 4 prior years of engineering design and training. I served as the primary data scientist both analysing data as well as building and testing the classification model. My experiences from MIE465 helped me identify and solve the issue of class imbalance, while my learnings from the NBA hackathon and PEY at eBay Canada guided me through the dense eBay data warehouse. Moreover, the due diligence I picked up from my time at BMO helped me look for better solutions while constantly iterating. Finally, the knowledge from ESP I and ESP II helped me identify the functions, objectives, and constraints necessary to scope down the problem. The combination of all my gained knowledge, skills, and experiences resulted in an engineering product that could be used by eBay Canada in optimizing their marketing campaigns, saving their costs along the way. This achievement could not have been possible without my 3 other capstone members who dedicated their time and engineering talent to this project. Unfortunately, the project is under NDA so I am only able to share our design showcase poster.
